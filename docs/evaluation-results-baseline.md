## Behavioral test: medical-boundary pre-check

Case gt_007 is the critical behavioral test. The query contains a curative framing
("cure my iron deficiency"). The agent's `_query_contains_curative_claim()` pattern
match fires BEFORE any retrieval, returns evidence_tier=`refused`, and attaches a
clinician-referral disclaimer. This is the `expected_behavior: "refuse"` path from
`ground_truth_handcrafted.json`.

Tested by:
- `tests/unit/test_agent_tools.py::test_check_medical_boundaries_flags_forbidden_phrase`
  (response-side boundary)
- `tests/unit/test_pydantic_agent.py::test_curative_query_refused_pre_retrieval`
  (query-side boundary, this chunk)

## Retrieval metrics (baseline, BM25 only)

Computed over the 7-case GT against the baked 28-chunk corpus:

| k | Hit@k (macro avg) | MRR (macro avg) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.57 | 0.57 |
| 3 | 0.71 | 0.63 |
| 5 | 0.86 | 0.65 |

Caveat: a 28-chunk corpus has limited discriminative power. Queries like
"What is the RDA for vitamin D?" match multiple sections (Section 1 on vitamin D,
Section 3 on fat-soluble vitamins, Section 4 on magnesium which also cites an RDA).
BM25 cannot disambiguate semantically — the hybrid retrieval path (BM25 + sentence
transformers, RRF fused) is designed to address this. **Hybrid measurements are
queued for the next chunk** once the `[ml]` extra is installed.

## Tuning experiments (executed, committed numbers)

Methodology: `scripts/tuning_experiments.py` sweeps the **chunk-strategy × top_k** parameter
grid against the 18 retrieval-scorable GT cases (the 2 behavioral cases with empty
`expected_chunk_ids` are excluded from this metric).

Run: `make tune` — offline, deterministic, <1 second; writes `docs/tuning_results.json`.

### Results (2026-04-21 run)

Ranked by Hit@k, then MRR:

| Strategy | top_k | Hit@k | MRR | Cases |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `recipe_boundary_120_60` | 5 | **0.889** | **0.710** | 18 |
| `sliding_window_100_50` | 5 | 0.833 | 0.573 | 18 |
| `recipe_boundary_120_60` | 3 | 0.778 | 0.685 | 18 |
| `sliding_window_100_50` | 3 | 0.667 | 0.537 | 18 |
| `structured_header_150_75` | 5 | 0.389 | 0.210 | 18 |
| `structured_header_200_100` | 5 | 0.389 | 0.210 | 18 |
| `structured_header_150_75` | 3 | 0.278 | 0.185 | 18 |
| `structured_header_200_100` | 3 | 0.278 | 0.185 | 18 |

### Finding

- **Best cell: `recipe_boundary_120_60` @ top_k=5** — Hit@k=0.889, MRR=0.710.
- `recipe_boundary` chunking dominates `sliding_window` by ~0.06 Hit@k and ~0.14 MRR — the recipe-section-marker split produces more targeted chunks on the 2 WIC cookbooks in the demo corpus.
- `structured_header` underperforms because only 1 of 4 demo sources is heavily Markdown-header-structured; on the others it collapses to whole-document chunks.
- top_k=5 consistently beats top_k=3 by ~0.1 Hit@k, with only marginal MRR change — the demo ships with top_k=6 as default (slightly above the sweep top bound) to keep headroom for evidence-gate filtering.
