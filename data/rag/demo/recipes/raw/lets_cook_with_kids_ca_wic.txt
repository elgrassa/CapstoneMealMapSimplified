## Recipe 1 — Banana Oat Pancakes

Ingredients:
- 1 ripe banana, mashed
- 1 cup rolled oats, blended into flour
- 1 large egg
- 3/4 cup low-fat milk
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
- Cooking oil for the pan

Servings: 4 small pancakes.

Method:
1. Mix all ingredients in a bowl until smooth (older children can whisk).
2. Heat a lightly oiled skillet over medium heat.
3. Pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake; cook 2 minutes per side.

Nutrition per serving: about 170 kcal, 6 g protein, 27 g carbohydrates, 4 g fat, 3 g fiber.

Kid-friendly tip: let children mash the banana and count out the oats — cooking together builds food familiarity and appetite regulation.

Allergens to note (EU-14 relevant): contains gluten (oats are gluten-contaminated unless certified gluten-free), dairy (milk), eggs.

## Recipe 2 — Veggie-Loaded Tomato Pasta

Ingredients:
- 225 g pasta (wheat or gluten-free)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 carrot, grated
- 1 zucchini, grated
- 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes, no salt added
- 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4.

Method:
1. Cook pasta according to package directions.
2. Sauté the onion, garlic, carrot, and zucchini in olive oil for 8 minutes.
3. Stir in tomatoes and herbs; simmer 10 minutes.
4. Toss with pasta.

Nutrition per serving: about 310 kcal, 10 g protein, 58 g carbohydrates, 5 g fat, 6 g fiber.

Low-cost tip: vegetable bulking (carrots, zucchini) stretches the sauce and increases fiber while keeping food costs low. Appropriate for children aged 1 year and above.

Allergens to note (EU-14 relevant): contains gluten unless a gluten-free pasta is used.
