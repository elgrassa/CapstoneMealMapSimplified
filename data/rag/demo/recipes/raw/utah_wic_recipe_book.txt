## Recipe 1 — Baked Chicken with Vegetables

Ingredients:
- 4 chicken thighs, skinless
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (no added salt)
- 4 carrots, sliced
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 2 medium potatoes, cubed

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Preheat the oven to 190 degrees C (375 F).
2. Arrange the vegetables in the bottom of a baking dish.
3. Season the chicken with garlic, thyme, and pepper; place on top of the vegetables.
4. Drizzle olive oil over everything.
5. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes.

Nutrition per serving: about 320 kcal, 22 g protein, 28 g carbohydrates, 12 g fat, low sodium.

This recipe is family-friendly, low in sodium, and appropriate for children aged 1 year and above.

## Recipe 2 — Low-Sodium Pasta Primavera

Ingredients:
- 225 g whole-wheat pasta
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, broccoli)
- 1/4 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
- Fresh basil, chopped
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions; drain.
2. In a large pan, sauté garlic in olive oil for 30 seconds.
3. Add vegetables and broth; cook for 5 minutes until tender-crisp.
4. Toss pasta with vegetables; top with basil and black pepper.

Nutrition per serving: about 290 kcal, 11 g protein, 55 g carbohydrates, 5 g fat. No added salt.

Whole-grain pasta provides fiber that supports digestive health; minimizing added sodium supports heart health for the whole family.

## Recipe 3 — Quick Bean Soup

Ingredients:
- 2 cups cooked white beans (canned, rinsed to reduce sodium)
- 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 carrots, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a pot; sauté onion, carrots, and celery for 5 minutes.
2. Add beans, broth, and oregano; bring to a simmer.
3. Simmer for 20 minutes; season with pepper.

Nutrition per serving: about 210 kcal, 10 g protein, 34 g carbohydrates, 5 g fat, 9 g dietary fiber.

Beans are a low-cost source of plant protein and iron. Pair with a source of vitamin C (a tomato garnish, a squeeze of lemon, or bell peppers) to improve non-heme iron absorption.
