## Recipe 1 — Lemon-Herb Baked Salmon with Broccoli

Ingredients:
- 4 salmon fillets (about 450 g total)
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 lemon, sliced
- 1 teaspoon dried dill
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees C (400 F).
2. Arrange salmon fillets and broccoli on a baking sheet.
3. Drizzle olive oil over everything; season with dill, garlic powder, and pepper.
4. Top each fillet with lemon slices.
5. Bake for 15 minutes until salmon flakes easily with a fork.

Nutrition per serving: about 310 kcal, 30 g protein, 6 g carbohydrates, 18 g fat, 2 g fiber.

Suitable for toddlers (deboned, cut into small pieces). Contains fish (EU-14 allergen). Dairy-free, peanut-free, gluten-free.

## Recipe 2 — Lentil Stew with Carrots and Spinach

Ingredients:
- 1 cup dry lentils, rinsed
- 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 2 carrots, diced
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cups fresh spinach
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a pot; sauté onion and carrots for 5 minutes.
2. Add lentils, broth, and cumin; bring to a simmer.
3. Cover and cook for 20 minutes until lentils are tender.
4. Stir in spinach and let wilt for 2 minutes.

Nutrition per serving: about 220 kcal, 12 g protein, 32 g carbohydrates, 5 g fat, 10 g fiber.

Toddler-friendly mashed texture. High in iron; pair with a vitamin C source (tomato, citrus) to improve non-heme iron absorption. Dairy-free, peanut-free.

## Recipe 3 — Brown Rice with Sweet Potato and Black Beans

Ingredients:
- 1 cup brown rice (long-grain)
- 2 cups water
- 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and cubed
- 1 can (400 g) black beans, rinsed
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. While rice cooks, roast sweet potato cubes at 200 degrees C (400 F) with olive oil for 20 minutes.
3. Warm black beans with cumin and paprika.
4. Combine rice, sweet potato, and beans in a bowl.

Nutrition per serving: about 340 kcal, 11 g protein, 60 g carbohydrates, 6 g fat, 11 g fiber.

Toddler-friendly (cut sweet potato small). Dairy-free, peanut-free, gluten-free.

## Recipe 4 — Vegetable and Egg Fried Rice

Ingredients:
- 2 cups cooked brown rice (cold, day-old is best)
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1/2 cup frozen peas
- 2 carrots, diced small
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Heat olive oil in a large pan or wok; sauté garlic and carrots for 3 minutes.
2. Push vegetables to one side; pour beaten eggs into the empty side and scramble.
3. Add rice and peas; stir to combine.
4. Drizzle soy sauce and pepper; toss for 2 minutes.

Nutrition per serving: about 280 kcal, 10 g protein, 42 g carbohydrates, 8 g fat, 4 g fiber.

Toddler-friendly. Contains eggs (EU-14 allergen). Contains gluten if using regular soy sauce. Dairy-free, peanut-free.

## Recipe 5 — Chickpea and Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:
- 1 can (400 g) chickpeas, rinsed
- 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 2 carrots, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 can (400 g) diced tomatoes
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- Black pepper to taste

Servings: 4

Method:
1. Heat olive oil; sauté onion, carrots, and celery for 5 minutes.
2. Add chickpeas, tomatoes, broth, oregano, and basil; bring to a simmer.
3. Cover and cook for 15 minutes until vegetables are tender.
4. Season with pepper.

Nutrition per serving: about 230 kcal, 10 g protein, 36 g carbohydrates, 6 g fat, 9 g fiber.

Toddler-friendly. Dairy-free, peanut-free, gluten-free.
